About

Building machines that
know what they don't know

I am a researcher in Trustworthy AI and Machine Learning Security, currently pursuing a PhD at Ulm University in collaboration with Huawei Research (Paris & Munich). My work sits at the intersection of formal reasoning, probabilistic uncertainty, and real-world ML deployments.

My core research integrates Subjective Logic and Bayesian Deep Learning into neural networks — enabling models to reason explicitly about their own uncertainty, propagate trust through multi-agent systems, and remain interpretable under adversarial conditions.

Beyond academia, I have worked at Huawei, Nokia Bell Labs, Mithril Security, and INRIA — building production-grade ML systems and defenses against real-world attacks. I contribute to EU HORIZON projects CONNECT, CASTOR, and DUCA.
